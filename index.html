Daydreaming To Escape

es·cap·ism

/əˈskāpˌizəm/

noun

the tendency to seek distraction and relief from unpleasant realities, especially by seeking entertainment or engaging in fantasy.

Sometimes the world can be unkind, depressing, or tiring. One way I run away from my problems is by vividly daydreaming.

Alternate Realities I Daydream About

The Well-Off Creative

I'm a successful writer/artist/designer who designed a whimsical house and am giving a house tour to Wendy Goodman from New York Magazine.

The Cottage Hermit

I'm a remote freelancer who lives in a secluded cottage by a creek buried deep in the forest and am working in my garden.

The Trustfund Scientist

I'm a wealthy heiress and biologist who founded a combined animal sanctuary, zoo, aquarium, and natural history museum and am studying botany.

The Better Clone

I'm actually just me, but better. I don't feel bothered, insecure, or anxious and am achieving all my dreams.

Parting Words

Thanks for taking a glimpse into my mind.

You're much better off not daydreaming if you have the ability to make any piece of your dreams into reality.

Byyyyyyye.
